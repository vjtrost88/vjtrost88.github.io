The shaded regions represent one standard deviation away from the mean. Thus, from the empirical rule, we can say that we are confident 67% of the true temperatures fall within that range. And from this figure, we can see that the most variabililty appears to happen in the beginning months of the year, when winter is in full swing. The tightest points are in the summer, suggesting there is less variability in temperatures during the summer. But it is also important to note the obvious observations from this figure, and that is that Ann Arbor, Michigan seems to have relatively hotter summers than State College, while our winters tend to stay comparable! html <- function(file){
  bool <- FALSE
  lines <- readLines(file)
  for(line in 1:length(lines)){
    if(grepl("<HTML>", lines[line]) || grepl("</HTML>", lines[line])){
      bool <- TRUE
      break
    }
  }
  return(bool)
}
